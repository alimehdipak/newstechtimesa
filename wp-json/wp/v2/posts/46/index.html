Harry Potter star Tom Felton has spoken of his \u201cshame\u201d over the way he and his castmates treated Emma Watson on the set of the first film.<\/p>\n

Tom played Draco Malfoy in all eight instalments of the Harry Potter franchise, and admitted that in the early years of the series, the Gyryffindor\/Slytherin divide spilled over into the real world.<\/p>\n

\u201cI suppose we thought we were a bit cooler,\u201d the actor wrote in his new memoir Beyond The Wand: The Magic And Mayhem Of Growing Up A Wizard.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe\u2019d spend our free time together listening to rap music \u2013 Wu-Tang, Biggie, 2Pac.\u201d<\/p>\n

Tom went on to recall an incident in which a nine-year-old Emma \u2013 the only girl in the film\u2019s main principal cast, who is three years his junior \u2013 had invited her co-stars to watch her perform a dance routine in her dressing room.

\u201cWe were predictably dismissive,\u201d he wrote. \u201cWe sniggered our way down to Emma\u2019s show, and the sniggers grew louder as she danced.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe were just being s\u043d\u03b9\u0442ty boys, largely out of awkwardness and because we thought taking the piss was cool, but Emma was visibly upset by our thoughtless reaction. I did feel like a bit of a dick, and rightly so.\u201d
\n
\nEventually, Tom apologised to Emma for his behaviour, and they \u201cmoved on\u201d, although he says he still feels pangs of shame when he thinks of the incident.<\/p>\n

He said: \u201cIt was just a stupid, teenage act of thoughtlessness, the sort of thing that happens every day. So why does that moment stick in my memory? Why is it so painful for me to recall?<\/p>\n

\u201cThe answer, I think, is that I\u2019ve grown to understand with the p\u1d00ssing of the years that of all of us, Emma had the most to deal with, the most difficult situation to negotiate, and from the earliest age.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cThe last thing she needed,\u201d he added. \u201cIn an environment that should have been \u2013 and normally was \u2013 safe and friendly and familial, was Josh [Gerdman, who played Goyle] and me laughing at her dance.
\n
\n\u201cThat\u2019s why I feel ashamed by the memory of our behaviour. And that\u2019s why I\u2019m glad that our friendship did not founder on the rocks of my insensitivity, but became something deeper. A touchstone for both of our lives.\u201d<\/p>\n
